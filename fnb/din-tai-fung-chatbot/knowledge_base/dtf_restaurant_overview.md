# Din Tai Fung — Restaurant Overview

## Brand Story

Din Tai Fung was founded in 1958 by Yang Bing-Yi in Taipei, Taiwan. Originally a cooking oil retail business, it pivoted to become a restaurant serving its now-famous Steamed Pork Xiao Long Bao (soup dumplings) in 1972.

The restaurant rose to international prominence when it was named one of the Top 10 Restaurants in the World by the New York Times in 1993. This recognition turned Din Tai Fung from a Taipei landmark into a global dining destination.

## Michelin Recognition

Din Tai Fung Hong Kong has held one Michelin star consecutively since 2009, making it one of the most celebrated dim sum restaurants in the world. The Singapore outlets, while not individually starred, operate to the same quality and craft standards.

## The 18 Pleats — Signature Craftsmanship

Every Steamed Pork Xiao Long Bao (XLB) at Din Tai Fung is hand-crafted with exactly 18 pleats. This is not a marketing claim — it is a rigorous quality standard enforced across every outlet worldwide. Dumplings that do not meet the 18-pleat standard are not served.

The dough is hand-rolled to a precise thickness. The filling uses premium pork and natural aspic (gelatinised broth) that liquefies into soup when steamed. Each dumpling weighs 21g.

## Culinary Philosophy

Din Tai Fung's cooking philosophy centres on precision, freshness, and consistency. Every dish is prepared fresh — no frozen items. Open kitchens at most outlets allow diners to watch the dumpling-makers at work.

The restaurant does not take shortcuts: premium ingredients, strict portion weights, and exacting preparation standards are non-negotiable at every location worldwide.
