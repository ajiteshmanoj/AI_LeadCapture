# Din Tai Fung Singapore — Frequently Asked Questions

## Dietary & Halal

**Q: Is Din Tai Fung Singapore Halal-certified?**
A: No. Din Tai Fung Singapore is NOT Halal-certified and does not hold any certification from MUIS or any other Halal certifying body. Our kitchen uses pork products, including lard in some doughs. We are unable to provide any Halal guarantees for any of our dishes.

**Q: Do you have vegetarian options?**
A: Yes. We offer Steamed Vegetable & Mushroom Dumplings, Vegetarian Fried Rice, Sesame Noodles, Cucumber Salad, Seaweed Tofu, and some soups without meat. However, all dishes are prepared in a shared kitchen where pork and other allergens are used. Guests with severe allergies should speak to our staff directly before ordering.

**Q: Are your dumplings made with lard?**
A: Some of our dough preparations use lard. If you have dietary restrictions related to pork, please be aware that we cannot guarantee that any item is free from pork derivatives.

**Q: Can I request a pork-free meal?**
A: Our kitchen is not set up for fully pork-free preparation. While we have dishes that do not contain pork as a listed ingredient (e.g., Braised Beef Noodle, Shrimp Fried Rice, some vegetarian items), cross-contamination in a shared kitchen cannot be ruled out.

**Q: Do you cater to guests with seafood/shellfish allergies?**
A: Please inform our staff of all allergies when you are seated. We will advise on ingredients to the best of our knowledge, but cannot guarantee allergen-free preparation in a shared kitchen environment.

## Reservations

**Q: Do you accept reservations?**
A: Yes, for groups of 5 or more at all outlets (except Changi Airport T3). Smaller groups are welcome to walk in and join our queue. You can make a reservation via this chat, by calling the outlet, or by visiting us directly.

**Q: How do I make a reservation via chat?**
A: Just tell our chatbot Mei the number of guests, your preferred outlet, date and time, your name, and contact number. She'll check availability and confirm your booking.

**Q: Can I make a same-day reservation?**
A: Yes, subject to availability. Contact the outlet directly or use the chatbot to check.

**Q: What is the largest group you can accommodate at a standard table?**
A: Standard tables accommodate groups of up to 8 guests. For 9–20 guests, we recommend enquiring about our Private Dining Room at Paragon.

**Q: How do I book the Private Dining Room?**
A: The Private Dining Room is at our Paragon outlet and fits 10–20 guests. A refundable $50 deposit is required. Contact us at least 3 days in advance at +65 6836 8336 or reservations@dintaifung.com.sg, or ask Mei via this chat.

**Q: Is there a deposit for group bookings?**
A: For standard reservations (5–9 pax), no deposit is required. For the Private Dining Room (10–20 pax), a $50 refundable deposit per table is required.

## Menu & Food

**Q: What is your signature dish?**
A: Our most iconic dish is the Steamed Pork Xiao Long Bao (小籠包), or XLB — hand-crafted soup dumplings with exactly 18 pleats each. Each dumpling weighs 21g and contains pork and natural aspic broth. Other signatures include Truffle & Pork XLB, Pan-Fried Pork Dumplings, and Braised Beef Noodle Soup.

**Q: What is inside the Xiao Long Bao?**
A: Our Steamed Pork XLB contains seasoned pork mince and natural aspic (gelatinised stock). When steamed, the aspic melts into hot soup inside the dumpling. Our Crab Roe variety adds fresh crab roe; the Truffle variety adds black truffle.

**Q: Do you have a children's menu?**
A: We do not have a separate children's menu. Our steamed dumplings, fried rice, and noodle dishes are popular with children. High chairs are available on request.

**Q: How spicy are your dishes?**
A: Most of our dishes are mild. Spicy options include the Spicy Braised Beef Noodle Soup and Spicy Wontons in Chilli Oil. Our chilli sauce served at the table is moderately spicy — staff can advise.

**Q: Can I see the full menu with prices?**
A: Our full menu is available at all outlets. Prices may vary slightly by location and are subject to 10% service charge and GST. Ask Mei for an overview or check the in-restaurant menu.

## Outlets & Hours

**Q: Where are your Singapore outlets?**
A: We have 7 outlets in Singapore: Paragon, ION Orchard, VivoCity, Jurong Point, Raffles City, Century Square (Tampines), and Changi Airport Terminal 3.

**Q: What are your opening hours?**
A: Most outlets open at 11:00am and close at 10:00pm daily. Changi Airport T3 opens at 7:00am. Last seating is typically 9:00–9:30pm. Check with individual outlets for holiday hours.

**Q: Do I need to queue? How long is the wait?**
A: Walk-in groups of 4 or fewer join our queue. During peak hours (weekday lunch 12–1:30pm, weekend lunch 11:30am–2pm, and all evenings from 7pm), expect 20–45 minutes at busy outlets like ION Orchard and Paragon. Reserving in advance avoids the queue entirely for groups of 5+.

**Q: Which outlet is closest to the MRT?**
A: ION Orchard has a direct basement connection to Orchard MRT with no rain exposure. VivoCity connects directly to HarbourFront MRT. Raffles City connects to City Hall MRT. Paragon is a 3-minute walk from Somerset MRT.

## Payment & Service

**Q: What payment methods do you accept?**
A: We accept Visa, Mastercard, Amex, JCB, PayNow, GrabPay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and cash. A 10% service charge and prevailing GST apply to all bills.

**Q: Can I request a specific table or seating arrangement?**
A: We take note of seating preferences (e.g., near the kitchen for dumpling-watching, window seat, wheelchair-accessible) but cannot guarantee specific tables. Please mention your preference when booking.

**Q: Do you offer takeaway or delivery?**
A: Takeaway is available at all outlets — simply order at the counter or inform your server. Delivery is available via major third-party platforms (GrabFood, Foodpanda). Note that dumplings are best eaten fresh; we recommend consuming takeaway XLBs within 15 minutes.

**Q: Is there parking near your outlets?**
A: All our outlets are within major malls that have paid parking. ION Orchard, Paragon, VivoCity, and Raffles City all have direct MRT access, which we recommend during peak hours.
