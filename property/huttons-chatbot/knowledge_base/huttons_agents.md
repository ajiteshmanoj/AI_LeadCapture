# Huttons Asia — Agent Guide & Buyer Representation

## How Huttons Matches Leads to Agents

Huttons uses a district-specialisation model to ensure buyers are matched with an agent who has deep expertise in their target area and property type.

### Matching Criteria
1. **District specialisation**: Each agent builds domain knowledge in specific districts (e.g. D20 Bishan/Thomson, D18 Tampines, D22 Jurong)
2. **Property type expertise**: HDB resale, private condo, landed, commercial, new launches — some agents specialise in one or two segments
3. **Language**: Agents are matched by preferred language (English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil, and other languages available)
4. **Availability**: Agents who are available for your preferred viewing timeline

### How to Get Matched
When you speak with James (our chatbot), he will:
1. Collect your property preferences (type, district, budget, citizenship status, timeline)
2. Qualify your requirements
3. Book a viewing or connect you with a specialist agent

## CEA Registration Requirement

All Huttons real estate agents (salespersons) must be:
- Registered with the **Council for Estate Agencies (CEA)**
- Affiliated with Huttons Asia as their registered estate agency
- In possession of a valid CEA Registration Certificate (renewed annually)

You can verify any agent's CEA registration at: **www.cea.gov.sg/public-register**

Huttons has zero tolerance for unethical practices. Report concerns to Huttons compliance or directly to CEA.

## Buyer Representation — What Does a Buyer's Agent Do?

For **property sales**, the seller pays the agent commission (typically 1–2% of sale price). Buyers do not pay commission. The buyer's agent represents your interests at no cost to you.

What your Huttons buyer's agent provides:
1. **Market analysis**: Comparable transactions, price trend data (URA Realis, SRX)
2. **Property shortlisting**: Identifies properties matching your criteria that you may not find on portals
3. **Viewing coordination**: Schedules viewings with seller's agent, accompanies you
4. **Negotiation**: Represents you in price negotiation
5. **Documentation**: Guides you through OTP, SPA, loan documents
6. **Professional network**: Refers lawyers, mortgage brokers, renovation contractors
7. **Post-sale support**: Tenant sourcing if investment, rental management referral

## What to Expect at a Viewing

### Before the Viewing
- Confirm address and parking availability
- Bring: NRIC (may be required), your list of questions, a phone for photos (ask permission)
- Our agent will meet you at the property

### During the Viewing
Key things to check:
1. **Direction/facing**: North-South facing means less direct afternoon sun
2. **Natural light**: Are rooms bright? Any light wells blocked?
3. **Ventilation**: Cross-ventilation in bedrooms and living areas
4. **Water pressure**: Test taps, check under sinks for pipe condition
5. **Electrical**: Check switch panel age, sufficient power points
6. **Structural**: Look for cracks in walls/ceiling, staining (past water leaks)
7. **Windows and doors**: Open smoothly, seals intact
8. **Noise**: Listen for MRT/highway/neighbour noise
9. **Mobile signal**: Check connectivity in all rooms
10. **View**: What's directly outside each window?
11. **Condo facilities** (if applicable): Pool condition, gym equipment, lift wait time, security

### Questions to Ask the Seller's Agent
- Why is the seller selling?
- How long has the property been on the market?
- Have there been any renovations? (get documentation)
- Any pending maintenance or assessments (for strata)?
- What is the current rental yield (for investment)?
- What's included/excluded in the sale price?

## Buyer's Checklist

Before making an offer:
- [ ] Obtained HFE letter (HDB) or bank pre-approval letter (private)
- [ ] Calculated total acquisition cost (price + BSD + ABSD + legal fees + reno)
- [ ] Verified CEA registration of agents involved
- [ ] Checked URA or HDB transaction data for comparable prices
- [ ] Attended at least 2-3 viewings of comparable properties
- [ ] Appointed conveyancing lawyer (before exercising OTP)
- [ ] Arranged mortgage broker consultation (if financing)
