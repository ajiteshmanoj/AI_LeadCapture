# Huttons Asia — Frequently Asked Questions

---

## 1. Do I need to pay the agent commission?

**For property sales**: Buyers do not pay commission. The seller pays the buyer's agent commission (typically 1–2% of the sale price). You get full buyer representation at no cost to you.

**For rentals**: The tenant typically pays 0.5 to 1 month's rent as commission to the tenant's agent. The landlord pays separately for the landlord's agent. Some landlords absorb the tenant's commission — ask your Huttons agent.

## 2. What is ABSD and how much do I pay?

ABSD (Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty) is a tax on residential property purchases in Singapore, paid on top of standard Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD).

| Profile | 1st Residential Property | 2nd | 3rd+ |
|---------|--------------------------|-----|------|
| Singapore Citizen | 0% | 20% | 30% |
| Permanent Resident | 5% | 30% | 35% |
| Foreigner | 60% | 60% | 60% |

Example: A Singapore Citizen buying a $1.5M condo as their first property pays 0% ABSD. A Foreigner buying the same pays $900,000 in ABSD alone.

---

## 3. Can foreigners buy HDB flats?

No. Foreigners are **not allowed to purchase HDB flats** (neither BTO nor resale). HDB flats are reserved for Singapore Citizens and (resale only) Permanent Residents with an eligible family nucleus.

Foreigners may purchase:
- Private condominiums
- Commercial and industrial properties
- Landed property at Sentosa Cove (with SLA approval)

---

## 4. What is COV (Cash Over Valuation)?

COV refers to the amount a buyer pays above the HDB's official valuation of a resale flat. HDB conducts a valuation during the OTP period; if the agreed price exceeds that valuation, the difference (COV) must be paid fully in cash (CPF cannot be used for COV).

Example: Agreed price = $700,000. HDB valuation = $650,000. COV = $50,000 (cash only).

---

## 5. How long does an HDB resale transaction take?

The typical HDB resale timeline from offer to key collection is **8–12 weeks**:
- HFE letter: 30 working days (apply before searching)
- Negotiation and OTP: 1–2 weeks
- OTP validity: 21 days
- HDB resale application processing: ~8 weeks
- HDB completion appointment: scheduled by HDB

---

## 6. Can I use CPF to pay for private property?

Yes, Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents can use CPF Ordinary Account (OA) savings for private property:
- Down payment (minimum 5% cash; remainder can be CPF + loan)
- Monthly mortgage installments
- Subject to the property's remaining lease (property must last until buyer turns 95 for full CPF usage)
- Withdrawal Limit: Up to 120% of Valuation Limit for private property

Foreigners cannot use CPF.

---

## 7. What is BSD (Buyer's Stamp Duty)?

BSD is a tax paid by all property buyers in Singapore, calculated on the higher of purchase price or market value:

| Amount | Rate |
|--------|------|
| First $180,000 | 1% |
| Next $180,000 | 2% |
| Next $640,000 | 3% |
| Next $500,000 | 4% |
| Next $1,500,000 | 5% |
| Remainder above $3M | 6% |

BSD is payable within 14 days of executing the Option to Purchase. Late payment incurs penalties.

## 8. Freehold vs 99-year leasehold — which is better?

**Freehold**: Ownership in perpetuity. Generally commands a price premium (10–20% higher). Better capital preservation. Common in prime districts (D9, D10, D11) and older developments.

**99-year leasehold**: More affordable. CPF usage and bank loan eligibility reduce as remaining lease shortens (below 60 years). Government land sales (GLS) are mostly 99-year. New launches are often 99-year.

**Our agents' view**: For own-stay, lifestyle and location matter more than tenure. For investment, freehold properties typically hold value better over long horizons but leasehold delivers comparable rental yields. Buy based on your holding period.

## 9. What is PSF (Price Per Square Foot)?

PSF (or psf) is the price per square foot of a property, calculated as:
**PSF = Total Price ÷ Floor Area in Sqft**

It is the standard metric used in Singapore to compare property values across different unit sizes and developments.

Example: A 700 sqft unit at $1,000,000 = $1,429 psf. A 1,000 sqft unit in the same development at $1,350,000 = $1,350 psf. The larger unit is relatively cheaper on a psf basis.

Our agents will benchmark psf against recent transactions in the same development and surrounding area.

## 10. What is an OTP (Option to Purchase)?

An OTP is a legal agreement granting the buyer the exclusive right (but not obligation) to purchase a property at an agreed price within a specified period.

For private property:
- Buyer pays **1% option fee** (cash) to receive the OTP
- OTP is valid for **21 days**
- Buyer exercises by paying a further **4%** (cash or CPF) = 5% total
- If buyer doesn't exercise: forfeits 25% of the option fee

For HDB resale:
- Seller grants OTP after agreeing on price
- Buyer pays up to $1,000 option fee (cash)
- HDB conducts valuation during OTP period
- OTP valid for 21 days

## 11. Can a Permanent Resident buy HDB resale?

Yes, but with conditions:
- PR must form an eligible family nucleus (e.g. married to a Singapore Citizen, or PR married to another PR)
- PR must have held PR status for at least **3 years** before applying to buy HDB resale
- PR is NOT eligible for CPF Housing Grants
- PR buying together with SC as a couple: the SC can apply for grants

Single PRs cannot buy HDB resale (unlike single SC who can buy 2-room and below after age 35).

## 12. What is an en-bloc sale?

An en-bloc (collective) sale is when all owners in a development (or 80%+ by strata area and share value) vote to sell the entire development to a developer. The developer typically redevelops the site.

Key facts:
- Requires 80% majority consent (developments ≤ 10 years old need 90%)
- Sellers typically receive a premium above individual market value
- Huttons has an en-bloc and collective sales division

## 13. How do I know if a property is a good investment?

Key factors our agents help you assess:
1. **Location**: MRT proximity, schools, amenities — demand drivers
2. **Supply**: Are there many competing units in the area? New launches nearby?
3. **Tenure**: Freehold vs leasehold — affects long-term value
4. **Rental yield**: Annual rent / Property value × 100. Typical SG yield: 2.5–3.5%
5. **Developer track record** (for new launches)
6. **URA Master Plan**: Future developments in the precinct
7. **Entry price vs historical psf**: Are you buying at a discount or premium to recent transactions?

Our agents use URA Realis and SRX data for objective analysis.

---

## 14. What is TOP date?

TOP (Temporary Occupation Permit) is the date a new development is deemed ready for occupation — issued by the Building and Construction Authority (BCA) once the building passes safety and completion checks.

For new launch buyers:
- You only collect keys and begin occupying at TOP
- Construction takes 3–5 years from launch
- Progressive payment schedule runs from purchase to TOP
- After TOP, the development has a further 1–2 years to get CSC (Certificate of Statutory Completion) — full legal completion

## 16. Can I sell a new launch unit before TOP?

Yes — this is called a **sub-sale** (selling a unit before TOP under a valid SPA). Conditions:
- Seller needs buyer's Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) to have lapsed (if applicable)
- Developer's consent may be required
- Can be complex — consult our agents and a conveyancing lawyer

## 17. What facilities does a condo typically include?

Standard condominium facilities in Singapore typically include:
- Swimming pool (lap pool, leisure pool, children's pool)
- Gymnasium
- Clubhouse / function rooms
- BBQ pavilions
- Tennis court or sports facilities
- 24-hour security/guardhouse
- Covered parking

Premium developments may also have: Sky terrace, indoor pool, spa, concierge, cinema room, co-working spaces.

Maintenance fees (paid monthly) cover upkeep of all common facilities.

## 18. How do I negotiate property price?

Our agents assist with:
1. **Market analysis**: Recent comparable transactions (same development/nearby)
2. **Days on market**: Properties listed longer are often more negotiable
3. **Seller motivation**: Urgent sale, divorce, financial distress — can drive better terms
4. **Inspection findings**: Defects discovered can be used to negotiate price or request repairs
5. **Market conditions**: Buyer's market vs seller's market affects leverage

Huttons agents negotiate on your behalf professionally, without conflict of interest.

## 19. What is rental yield?

Rental yield measures the annual return on a property investment:

**Gross Rental Yield (%)** = (Annual Rental Income / Property Purchase Price) × 100

**Net Rental Yield** = (Annual Rental Income − Annual Expenses) / Property Purchase Price × 100

Expenses include: property tax, maintenance fee, insurance, management fees, vacancy periods.

Typical Singapore rental yield: 2.5%–3.5% gross for residential. Commercial properties can achieve 4%–6%.

## 20. Should I sell my current property before buying a new one?

This depends on your financial situation:

**If you sell first**:
- Avoid ABSD on second property (saves 20–30% for SC/PR)
- But risk being homeless during gap period
- May need bridging loan or temporary rental

**If you buy first**:
- Pay ABSD on second property
- Can apply for ABSD remission if you sell within 6 months of new property's completion (SC married couples only — specific conditions apply)
- Bridging loan bridges the gap

**Our recommendation**: Discuss with a Huttons agent and mortgage broker to model the costs. The ABSD remission scheme can make buying first viable for SC married couples in certain situations.
