# Raffles Medical Group — Frequently Asked Questions

## Appointments

**Q: How do I book an appointment?**
A: You can book through this chat, call your preferred clinic directly, or walk in during operating hours. Booking in advance helps reduce your waiting time.

**Q: Can I walk in without an appointment?**
A: Yes. Walk-ins are welcome at all Raffles Medical clinics during operating hours. However, appointment patients are generally seen first, so walk-in wait times may be longer, especially during peak hours (Mon–Fri mornings).

**Q: How early should I arrive for my appointment?**
A: Please arrive 10–15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time to complete registration. For health screenings, arrive at least 20 minutes early.

**Q: Can I book a same-day GP appointment?**
A: Yes, subject to availability. Contact the clinic by phone or via this chat for same-day slots.

**Q: How do I reschedule or cancel my appointment?**
A: Please call the clinic directly or notify us via this chat at least 24 hours before your appointment. Late cancellations for specialist and screening appointments may incur a fee.

**Q: Do I need a referral to see a specialist?**
A: Generally yes. Most specialist appointments at Raffles Medical require a referral letter from a GP (from Raffles Medical or elsewhere). Our GPs can refer you on the same day. Some specialties accept self-referrals — please confirm when booking.

## Fees and Payment

**Q: How much is a GP consultation?**
A: A standard GP consultation is $38. This excludes medication, blood tests, and other procedures if required.

**Q: How much is a specialist consultation?**
A: Specialist consultation fees range from $150 to $300 depending on the specialty and doctor. Please check with the clinic for exact fees.

**Q: What payment methods are accepted?**
A: Raffles Medical accepts cash, NETS, credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, AMEX), and corporate direct billing. Medisave and CHAS subsidies are accepted where eligible.

**Q: Can I use Medisave for my GP visit?**
A: No. Medisave cannot be used for standard GP consultations in Singapore. It can be used for approved specialist treatments, day surgery, hospitalisation, approved health screenings (Medisave500), and approved vaccinations.

**Q: Can I use Medisave for a specialist consultation?**
A: Yes, for certain approved conditions and procedures. Our staff can advise on eligibility when you register. Please bring your NRIC and ensure your Medisave account has sufficient funds.

**Q: Do you accept CHAS subsidies?**
A: Yes, at selected Raffles Medical GP clinics. Present your CHAS card at registration. Subsidies apply to GP consultations and selected chronic disease management.

## Health Screening

**Q: Do I need to fast before a health screening?**
A: For most executive health screening packages involving blood glucose and cholesterol tests, you should fast for at least 8–10 hours before your appointment. Water is fine. Please confirm fasting requirements when booking.

**Q: Can I use Medisave for health screening?**
A: Yes, Medisave can be used for approved health screening packages under the Medisave500 scheme (up to $500 per year). Our staff will advise on eligibility at registration.

## Teleconsultation

**Q: Is teleconsultation available?**
A: Yes. Raffles Connect offers video and phone teleconsultations for follow-up appointments, prescription renewals, and minor ailments. Book a teleconsult via this chat or through the Raffles Connect app.

**Q: Can teleconsultation replace a physical examination?**
A: No. Teleconsult is not suitable for emergencies, first-time consultations for complex conditions, or any condition requiring physical examination. For these, please attend in person.

## Clinical

**Q: What should I bring to my first visit?**
A: Bring your NRIC or passport, insurance card (if applicable), referral letter (for specialist appointments), a list of current medications, and any relevant past medical records or investigation results.

**Q: Can Raffles Medical treat work-related injuries?**
A: Yes. Raffles Medical can provide consultation and treatment for work injuries and issue medical certificates. Employers can be billed directly in some cases.

**Q: Does Raffles Medical issue MCs (medical certificates)?**
A: Yes. Doctors at Raffles Medical can issue MCs for eligible conditions during a consultation.

**Q: What happens if I need emergency care?**
A: For life-threatening emergencies, call 995 immediately. Raffles Hospital has a 24-hour Accident and Emergency (A&E) department at 585 North Bridge Road. For urgent but non-emergency concerns, ask our chat assistant to connect you to a nurse.

## Data and Privacy

**Q: How is my personal data used?**
A: Your data is collected for appointment management and clinical care only, in compliance with Singapore's PDPA. It is not sold or shared with third parties. See our privacy policy for details.

**Q: Is it safe to share my NRIC with this chatbot?**
A: Only share the last 4 characters of your NRIC (e.g. "123A") for verification purposes. Do not share your full NRIC in the chat.
